 📊 Fear vs Greed: How Market Emotions Impact Trading Behavior


This project started with a simple curiosity:
**Do traders actually behave differently when the market is fearful vs greedy?**

Instead of focusing only on prices, I wanted to explore the *human side of trading* — how emotions influence decisions, risk-taking, and outcomes.


## 🚀 Why I Built This

While learning data analytics, I noticed most projects focus only on numbers.
But in real markets, **psychology plays a huge role**.

So I decided to explore:

* How sentiment affects trading performance
* Whether traders take more risks during Greed
* And how Fear impacts consistency


## 🧠 What This Project Covers

* Merging **Fear & Greed Index** with trading data
* Classifying days into **Fear vs Greed**
* Analyzing:

  * PnL (Profit & Loss)
  * Win Rate
  * Trading Activity
* Comparing behavior across different market sentiments

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

* Python
* Pandas
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn

Simple tools, with a focus on extracting meaningful insights.


## 📊 Key Insights

* 📈 **Greed → More aggressive trading**
  Traders tend to take bigger risks and trade more frequently

* 📉 **Fear → Higher uncertainty**
  Performance becomes inconsistent due to volatility

* ⚖️ **Behavior changes with sentiment**
  Market mood clearly impacts decision-making


## 💡 Practical Takeaways

**During Fear markets**

* Reduce position size
* Focus on high-confidence trades
* Avoid overtrading

**During Greed markets**

* Don’t get carried away
* Book profits early
* Stick to risk management
